# Code Of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want the Actenon Kernel community to be open, constructive, and trustworthy for contributors, adopters, reviewers, and maintainers.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- showing empathy and respect toward other people
- giving and receiving constructive feedback
- focusing on what improves the public kernel, public specs, verifier edge, and conformance surface
- accepting accountability and learning from mistakes
- keeping technical disagreement specific, evidence-based, and professional

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- abusive, insulting, or demeaning language
- personal attacks or bad-faith escalation
- publishing private information without permission
- disruptive conduct that makes the project harder to participate in safely

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that are not aligned with this code of conduct.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it through the repository maintainer contact path or the hosting platform's private contact mechanisms when available.

If the issue is a security vulnerability, use the guidance in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead of a public issue.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to unacceptable behavior, including warning the offender, removing content, restricting participation, or banning contributors from project spaces.
